Differentials

All features computed as Team A minus Team B — capturing relative strength, not absolutes.

Baseline Features

Seed difference — the single most predictive feature. Win percentage, conference strength.

Efficiency Features

Four Factors: eFG%, TO%, ORB%, FTR. KenPom/BartTorvik adjusted efficiency. Points per possession.

Advanced Features

Momentum (actual vs. preseason KenPom), Elo, 538 rating, EvanMiya killshots, resume quality (Q1+Q2 wins).

Missing Values: Elo → 1500, Ratings → 0, Seeds → median. StandardScaler for LR; trees use raw values (scale-invariant).

The Cinderella Zone

11-seeds win 46.5% of games — nearly a coin flip. Four Elite Eight appearances, four Final Fours. The classic 5-vs-12 upset hits 35% of the time.

Historical Over- & Under-Performers

Over-performers: Michigan State and Syracuse have historically exceeded their seeding — MSU has reached 8 Final Fours since 1999, often as a 5-seed or lower. Loyola Chicago (2018, 11-seed Final Four) and VCU (2011, 11-seed Final Four) are classic Cinderella examples.

Under-performers: Virginia became the first 1-seed to lose to a 16-seed (UMBC, 2018). Kansas has multiple early exits despite top-4 seeds. Gonzaga earned high seeds repeatedly but didn't win a title until 2017 season and has had early stumbles as a top seed.

Why This Blend?

Key Takeaways

THE BOTTOM LINE

The Tournament Isn't Random

It's predictable if you look beyond seeds. Teams that peak late, shoot free throws under pressure, and move the ball are the ones cutting down nets.

Our model captures this with a Brier Score of 0.188 across 17 seasons of honest backtesting.

Brier Score Context

Our 0.188 honestly captures historically upset-heavy seasons (2011, 2014, 2023). Best folds reach 0.157. A "perfect" model in a sport with this variance would score ~0.160. Our model is robust over safe — designed for live conditions, not overfitting to calm years.

Methodology Safeguards

✓ Pre-tournament data only — zero leakage
✓ Season-based GroupKFold — no future data in training
✓ Symmetric matchup pairs — no ordering bias
✓ Isotonic calibration — probabilities match reality
✓ Seed-anchored LR baseline — tree overconfidence guard
! Probabilities capped [2.5%, 97.5%] — never predict certainty
! 33.3% Brier threshold for all upset decisions
