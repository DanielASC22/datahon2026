The Challenge

Can data predict tournament chaos? Using 17 seasons of historical data (2008–2025), we analyzed 1,070 tournament games to identify the measurable traits that separate deep runs from early exits — then projected those patterns onto the 2026 bracket.

What Actually Predicts Wins

The 3 most predictive features are not what casual fans expect:

  1. 1
    Momentum — Teams improving most from preseason KenPom projections are peaking at the right time. Late-season trajectory matters more than where you started.
  2. 2
    Free Throw % — In tournament pressure, games are decided at the line. Elite FT shooting is the strongest indicator of clutch execution.
  3. 3
    Assist Rate — Ball movement breaks down tournament defenses. Iso-heavy teams struggle against the tighter, more physical play of March.

Our Approach
Data
KenPom, BartTorvik, 538, EvanMiya, Elo ratings, conference stats — pre-tournament only to prevent data leakage.
Features
Four Factors (eFG%, TO%, ORB%, FTR), adjusted efficiency, momentum, Elo, resume quality — 48 total differentials.
Models
4-model ensemble: LightGBM + CatBoost blended with Logistic Regression and a seed-anchored baseline to prevent overconfidence.
Validation
Season-based cross-validation ensures the model never trains on future data. Isotonic calibration aligns probabilities with reality.

The tournament isn't random — it's predictable if you look beyond seeds. Teams that peak late, shoot free throws under pressure, and move the ball are the ones cutting down nets. Our model captures this with mathematical precision, delivering a Brier Score of 0.188 across 17 seasons of backtesting — giving bracket-makers a measurable, data-driven edge over chalk.
